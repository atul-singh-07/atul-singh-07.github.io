Atul Singh

I spend most of my time building small systems and trying to understand how things actually work — not just in theory, but in practice.

My interest started with electronics and hardware, but very quickly I realized that the real magic happens where hardware meets software. Writing code that controls something physical, watching it fail, fixing it, and seeing it work again is what keeps me engaged.

I enjoy experimenting with microcontrollers, sensors, and motors, and I’m particularly interested in how low-level code interacts with hardware. I don’t aim for perfect builds — I aim for learning why something behaves the way it does.

Most of what I know comes from building projects, breaking them, debugging connections, rewriting logic, and slowly improving things step by step. That process matters more to me than the final result.

Right now, I’m focused on strengthening my fundamentals in electronics and programming, while continuing to build hands-on projects that help me connect both worlds more clearly.

You can find my work and experiments on GitHub.
